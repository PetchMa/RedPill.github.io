The Technology

Understand How It Works

Brain Stimulation + State + Memory = Skills.

Our plan is to use multiple methods of stimuli to manipulate the mind to learn complex tasks. Currently, we’ve made tremendous progress in the fields of neurology and brain computer interfaces, many of which can be combined together to eventually create downloadable skills.

We've broken down the core components into three main categories, that being Brain Stimulation, State and Memory. Brain stimulation help allow the patient to mimic desired tasks. Then, the state of the mind is altered to allow retention of information. Finally, we commit the learnt skills into long term memory. Simple.

We invite you to learn more about our works in elevating the human mind.

Brain Stimulation

Inorder for us to begin 'writing' to the mind, we must know how the neurons of the brain work.

Connectomics

Connectomics is the field that is helpping us understand the neuro path ways of the brain. It’s currently done through chemical brain preservation, followed by 3D electron microscopy.

Optogenetics

After understanding the inner workings we need to stimulate the mind in specific areas. Optogenetics is a technique by which neurologists genetically modify neurons in the brain to be responsive to light through light-sensitive proteins. When a light is shined on those neurons, they stimulate the neurons to fire up. This effectively allows us to control and monitor individual neurons in the brain.

State

After stimulating the brain we need to help the user REMEMBER their learnings. By placing the user in a state of heightened neuroplasticity and synaptogenesis we can increase the speed of learning

TDCS

TCDS is also shown to be able to heighten creativity, improve the ability to learn mathematics and linguistics! Though it doesn’t transfer new information, it improves our ability to learn. It also promotes neuroplasticity.

Memory

Fusing skills and brain mappings with heightened states of learning would definitely help with skill retention, but what about storing long term memory?

Synaptogenesis

To go from one neuron to the next: the first neuron sends neurotransmitters, which bond to the gates of the second neuron. Neurotransmitters can open or close the gates. Those that open the gates allow the flow of ions, allowing communication between neurons
